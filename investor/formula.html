Formula & Logic

The math should feel short, legible, and useful. These are the formulas worth showing because they explain the product without drowning the reader in derivations.

1. Time correction

All downstream output depends on one thing first: converting civil birth data into a consistent astronomical reference. That means timezone handling, Julian date logic, and precision corrections before interpretation begins.

2. Fusion signal

The combined signal can be shown simply: Western contribution plus BaZi contribution plus Wu Xing contribution. Investors do not need every low-level constant. They need the model shape.

How to present it

  • Show only formulas that change product meaning.
  • Translate each one into a business sentence.
  • Never present equations without the investor takeaway next to them.
Good formula design is not about proving academic sophistication. It is about making Bazodiac look precise, readable, and intentionally engineered.
