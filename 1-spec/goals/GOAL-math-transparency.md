# GOAL-math-transparency: Every Number Must Be Realistic, Plausible, and Auditable

**Description**: BAZODIAC's core differentiator is mathematical rigor — "transparent mathematics, auditable, deterministic." This principle must be reflected on the insight page itself. Every number shown to investors — market sizes, formulas, persona economics, growth metrics — must be realistic, internally consistent, and traceable to a source or derivation. No vanity metrics. No inflated projections. No black boxes. This is what separates BAZODIAC from the astrology industry it critiques.

**Status**: Draft

**Priority**: Must-have

**Source stakeholder**: [STK-founder](../stakeholders.md), [STK-investor](../stakeholders.md), [STK-marc](../stakeholders.md)

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Market size figures ($2.2B digital / $12.8B total) are attributed to named research sources (e.g., Allied Market Research, Grand View Research) with publication year
- [ ] The FuFirE formula `Signal(s) = 0.375·W(s) + 0.375·B(s) + 0.25·X(s)` is mathematically correct: weights sum to 1.0, dimensional claims (R⁵, S⁴, L2-norm) are accurate
- [ ] Persona economic profiles (income, LTV estimates, CAC targets) are plausible against published benchmarks for the DACH region and app economy
- [ ] No number on the page is presented without either (a) a source citation, (b) a stated derivation method, or (c) an explicit "preliminary estimate" label
- [ ] The Coherence Index H is defined with its mathematical properties (range, interpretation) — not just displayed as a floating number
- [ ] Subscription pricing is presented as preliminary with appropriate framing (see [CON-subscription-not-onetime](../constraints/CON-subscription-not-onetime.md))
