# GOAL-premium-brand-perception: Signal "Deep Tech Meets Luxury" — Not "Another Astrology App"

**Description**: The visual and interaction design of the insight page must instantly communicate that BAZODIAC is a premium, technology-driven product — not a consumer entertainment app. The "Dark Luxury" design language (obsidian backgrounds, gold-leaf accents, glassmorphism, particle physics visualizations) must create an emotional response of sophistication, exclusivity, and intellectual depth. An investor should feel they are looking at a fintech or deep-tech product that happens to operate in the identity/astrology space — not an astrology product trying to look technical.

**Status**: Draft

**Priority**: Must-have

**Source stakeholder**: [STK-founder](../stakeholders.md), [STK-lena](../stakeholders.md)

## Success Criteria

- [ ] First-impression test: a viewer unfamiliar with the product should describe it as "tech/fintech/data" before "astrology/horoscope" based on visual design alone
- [ ] Interactive canvas visualizations (ConnectedVortex, ZodiacRing, FuFirE Radar) function smoothly at ≥30fps on standard hardware (MacBook Air M1, iPhone 13+)
- [ ] The design language is consistent across all sections: no section breaks the premium aesthetic with generic UI patterns, stock imagery, or template-looking layouts
- [ ] Typography follows the established hierarchy: ultra-light tracking-widest uppercase headlines, monospace for technical/mathematical content, clean sans-serif for body
- [ ] The page passes the "screenshot test" — any single-section screenshot could credibly appear in a TechCrunch or Wired article about deep-tech startups
- [ ] Micro-interactions (custom cursor, nebula lens, scroll-triggered text decode, parallax) reinforce the sense of a live, computed system — not a static pitch deck
- [ ] Mobile experience preserves premium feel with graceful degradation (reduced particle counts, simplified interactions) — not a broken desktop layout
