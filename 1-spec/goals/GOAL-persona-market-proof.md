# GOAL-persona-market-proof: Prove the Target Segment is Larger and More Monetizable Than Entertainment Astrology

**Description**: The insight page must shift the investor's mental model away from "another astrology app for believers" toward "a thinking-tool platform for the framework-curious premium segment." The four personas (Lena, Marc, Sophia, Kai) must demonstrate — with data and logic, not just narrative — that the agnostic, semi-spiritual, higher-income, higher-education segment is (a) underserved, (b) larger than commonly assumed, and (c) willing to pay significantly more than the average Co-Star user ($0 ARPU). This is the blue-ocean thesis made concrete.

**Status**: Draft

**Priority**: Must-have

**Source stakeholder**: [STK-founder](../stakeholders.md), [STK-investor](../stakeholders.md)

## Success Criteria

- [ ] The page presents the four personas with enough detail that an investor understands the segment without a verbal explanation
- [ ] Each persona card or section shows: archetype name, demographic snapshot, relationship to BAZODIAC (why they use it), monetization role (how they pay or create value), and a data-backed claim about segment size or behavior
- [ ] The "73% of Gen Z/Millennials use identity frameworks" claim is sourced and contextualized (which frameworks, what kind of usage, which surveys)
- [ ] The page explicitly contrasts the target segment against the entertainment-astrology segment: different motivations, different willingness to pay, different retention drivers
- [ ] The "0% BaZi penetration in West" blue-ocean claim is substantiated (e.g., app store search data, market reports showing no Western BaZi digital product at scale)
- [ ] Unit economics per persona (even if preliminary) are internally consistent: CAC < LTV for each segment, aggregate blended metrics are plausible
