# GOAL-risk-transparency: Show Investors That Risks Are Identified, Quantified, and Have Kill Criteria

**Description**: The insight page must communicate to investors that BAZODIAC's founding team is not naive about its risks — it has identified the two critical assumptions, diagnosed why each risk is real, built strategic defense lines, defined measurable KPIs with target/warning/kill thresholds, pre-committed to a 90-day validation cycle with non-negotiable kill criteria, and prepared concrete pivot scenarios. This transforms the investor perception from "founder who believes in the vision" to "founder who runs disciplined experiments." In early-stage investing, risk awareness is often more convincing than risk absence.

**Status**: Draft

**Priority**: Must-have

**Source stakeholder**: [STK-founder](../stakeholders.md), [STK-investor](../stakeholders.md)

## Success Criteria

- [ ] The page includes a dedicated section (or clearly integrated narrative) addressing BAZODIAC's two critical risks:
  1. **Substance vs. UX** — mathematical depth may not translate to perceived value (ASM-fufire-substance-over-cosmic-ux)
  2. **Premium WTP** — framework-curious segment may not pay more than entertainment users (ASM-framework-segment-higher-wtp)
- [ ] For each risk, the page communicates:
  - **Why the risk is real** — honest diagnosis, not hand-waving
  - **Strategic defense lines** — concrete mitigation strategies (Proof of Precision moment, Companion-First/Engine-Second, tiered pricing validation)
  - **Measurable KPIs** with specific targets (e.g., D30 retention ≥ 18%, Free→Essentials conversion ≥ 5%)
  - **Kill criteria** — conditions under which the team will pivot, and what the pivot looks like
  - **Fallback plans** — FuFirE as a Service (B2B API), volume pivot, tier compression
- [ ] The 90-day validation cycle is presented as a disciplined process with fixed review cadence (Day 14/30/60/90)
- [ ] The page communicates the key principle: "Kill criteria are committed before launch and cannot be changed during the cycle"
- [ ] The asset preservation message is clear: "The FuFirE Engine remains valuable in every scenario — only the packaging changes"
- [ ] An investor reading the risk section should conclude: "This founder knows what could go wrong and has a plan for each scenario"

## Why This Goal Exists

Most early-stage pitch materials hide risks or address them with vague "we'll iterate." This actively hurts credibility with experienced investors who know that:

1. Every startup has critical assumptions — hiding them signals naivety, not strength
2. The quality of kill criteria reveals the quality of the founder's thinking
3. Pre-committed pivot plans reduce investor risk because capital is not wasted on dead-end iteration
4. A founder who can articulate "here's when I'll pivot and what I'll pivot to" is dramatically more fundable than one who says "I believe in the vision"

The competitive analysis identified that FuFirE suffers from Overconfidence Bias — showing risk awareness directly counteracts this perception.
