# ASM-market-size-defensible: Market Size Figures Are Attributable to Credible Research

**Category**: Business

**Status**: Unverified

**Risk if wrong**: Medium — if the market figures cannot be attributed to named, credible research sources, the entire market opportunity section of the insight page loses credibility. Investors will dismiss unattributed market claims as "made up."

## Statement

The following market figures used across BAZODIAC materials are assumed to be attributable to credible, published research sources:

| Figure | Claimed scope | Needs source verification |
|--------|--------------|--------------------------|
| $14.3B (2024) | Global astrology total market | Yes — likely from market research aggregator |
| $25.6B (2034) | Global astrology projection | Yes — same source as above |
| 6.0% CAGR | Global total growth | Yes |
| $4.73B (2025) | Astrology apps segment | Yes |
| $11.71B (2030) | Apps segment projection | Yes |
| 20.2% CAGR | Apps segment growth | Yes |
| 73% | Gen Z/Millennials using identity frameworks | Yes — likely from YPulse, Morning Consult, or Pew |
| 82% | Chinese Gen Z believing in / using astrology | Yes — from the Metaphysische Ökonomie analysis |

## Rationale

The Metaphysische Ökonomie analysis provides the most detailed market data with apparent sourcing, but the original research report names (e.g., Allied Market Research, Grand View Research, Precedence Research) are not explicitly cited with report IDs or URLs. For investor-grade credibility, each figure must trace to a named report.

## Verification Plan

1. **Source audit**: For each figure, find the original research report, record the publisher name, report title, publication year, and page/section reference
2. **Cross-check**: Verify that at least 2 independent sources agree on order-of-magnitude for key figures (total market, apps segment, CAGR)
3. **Date freshness**: Ensure all cited figures are from 2023 or later publications
4. **Attribution format**: Decide how sources will be displayed on the insight page (footnotes, hover tooltips, or a "Sources" section)
