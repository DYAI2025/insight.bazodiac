# ASM-cac-under-three: CAC Below €3 is Achievable Through Viral Mechanics

**Category**: Business

**Status**: Unverified

**Risk if wrong**: Medium — if CAC is significantly higher than €3, the unit economics presented to investors become unrealistic. The Kai persona's role as "acquisition engine" weakens, and the blended LTV:CAC ratio may not support the subscription model.

## Statement

A Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) below €3 is achievable through organic/viral mechanics driven primarily by the Kai persona segment:

- **Signature-Morph animations** serve as shareable social status markers (scroll-stoppers in feeds)
- **Viral coefficient target k=0.8** — each user generates 0.8 additional users organically
- **"Your zodiac sign is only 30%"** curiosity hook devalues competitors and drives funnel entry
- **Quiz-driven IKEA effect** — user investment in sharpening their profile increases emotional attachment and sharing behavior

No paid advertising budget is required for initial traction under this model.

## Rationale

- The persona dossier positions Kai as the primary acquisition channel with explicit CAC <€3 target
- Viral mechanics in identity/personality products have precedent: 16Personalities (MBTI) achieved massive organic growth through shareable result cards
- The 29,000-particle Fusion Ring visualization is designed as a "scroll-stopper" in social feeds
- DACH market has lower social media advertising CPMs compared to US, supporting lower CAC

## Verification Plan

1. **Pre-launch viral test**: Create a minimal shareable Signature card (static, no full product needed) and measure organic sharing rate in target demographics
2. **k-coefficient measurement**: Track invite/share rates during beta to validate k=0.8 target
3. **CAC tracking from day 1**: Instrument analytics to measure true CAC per channel from soft launch
4. **Benchmark research**: Document CAC for comparable identity/personality apps (16Personalities, Human Design apps, Co-Star at launch)
