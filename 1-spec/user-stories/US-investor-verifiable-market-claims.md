# US-investor-verifiable-market-claims: Every Market Figure Has a Named Source

**As a** prospective investor, **I want** every market size, growth rate, and demographic statistic on the page to be attributed to a named research source with publication year, **so that** I can verify the claims during my due diligence and trust that the team is rigorous about data.

**Status**: Draft

**Priority**: Must-have

**Source stakeholder**: [STK-investor](../stakeholders.md), [STK-advisor](../stakeholders.md)

**Related goal**: [GOAL-math-transparency](../goals/GOAL-math-transparency.md)

## Acceptance Criteria

- Given I see a market size figure (e.g., "$14.3B global astrology market"), when I look for attribution, then I find a source name and year (e.g., "Source: Precedence Research, 2024") either inline, as a footnote, or via hover/tooltip
- Given I see a CAGR or growth projection, when I check the number, then the figure is consistent with the source's methodology (total market vs. apps segment vs. regional scope clearly labeled)
- Given I see the "73% of Gen Z/Millennials use identity frameworks" claim, when I look for the source, then I find a named survey or research report — not just an unattributed statistic
- Given I see subscription pricing or unit economics, when I check whether they're presented as facts or estimates, then preliminary figures are explicitly labeled as "preliminary estimate" or "benchmark — to be validated"
