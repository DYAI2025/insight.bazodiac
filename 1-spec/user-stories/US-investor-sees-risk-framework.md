# US-investor-sees-risk-framework: See Honest Risk Assessment With Defense Lines and Kill Criteria

**As a** prospective investor, **I want** to see that the founding team has identified its two critical risks, diagnosed why each is real, built strategic defense lines, and defined measurable KPIs with kill thresholds, **so that** I trust the team makes disciplined, data-driven decisions rather than running on belief alone.

**Status**: Must-have

**Priority**: Must-have

**Source stakeholder**: [STK-investor](../stakeholders.md)

**Related goal**: [GOAL-risk-transparency](../goals/GOAL-risk-transparency.md)

## Acceptance Criteria

- Given I reach the risk section, when I read it, then I see both critical risks clearly named:
  1. **Substance vs. UX risk**: Mathematical depth may not translate to perceived user value
  2. **Premium WTP risk**: Framework-curious segment may not pay more than entertainment users
- Given I read Risk 1, when I look for the response, then I see three defense lines: (1) Proof of Precision moment in onboarding, (2) Companion-First / Engine-Second strategy, (3) FaaS B2B fallback
- Given I read Risk 2, when I look for the response, then I see the three-tier pricing validation strategy (Free / Essentials €4.99 / Premium €9.99) with specific sub-hypotheses per tier
- Given I want to see accountability, when I look for KPIs, then I find at least 3 measurable metrics per risk with specific target, warning, and kill thresholds (e.g., "D30 Retention: target ≥18%, warning <12%, kill <7%")
- Given I want to see what happens if assumptions fail, when I look for pivot plans, then each risk has a concrete fallback: Risk 1 → UX simplification + B2B API; Risk 2 → volume pivot + tier compression
- Given I am evaluating founder quality, when I read the risk section, then I conclude: "This founder knows what could go wrong and has pre-committed to act on the data"
