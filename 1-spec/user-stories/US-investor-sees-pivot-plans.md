# US-investor-sees-pivot-plans: See Concrete Fallback Plans if Critical Assumptions Fail

**As a** prospective investor, **I want** to see that my capital is protected by concrete pivot scenarios if either critical assumption is falsified, **so that** I know the FuFirE Engine remains a valuable asset regardless of consumer market outcome — and that the team will pivot decisively, not burn cash on hope.

**Status**: Must-have

**Priority**: Must-have

**Source stakeholder**: [STK-investor](../stakeholders.md)

**Related goal**: [GOAL-risk-transparency](../goals/GOAL-risk-transparency.md)

## Acceptance Criteria

- Given I read the risk section, when I look for fallback plans, then I see at least two concrete pivot scenarios:
  1. **FuFirE as a Service (FaaS)**: B2B API licensing — other astrology apps (including Cosmic Fusion) use generic APIs; a more precise engine as API service serves the entire industry as infrastructure
  2. **Volume Pivot**: Switch to freemium model (€2.99/week or €39.99/year), compress features into single tier, monetize via volume and in-app purchases
- Given I want to understand when pivots trigger, when I look for conditions, then each pivot has a specific trigger tied to the 90-day kill criteria (not vague "if things don't work out")
- Given I am concerned about sunk cost bias, when I read the pivot section, then I find the principle: "Kill criteria are committed before launch and cannot be changed during the cycle"
- Given I want to understand asset preservation, when I read the fallbacks, then I find the message: "The FuFirE Engine remains valuable in every scenario — as consumer backend, as B2B API, or as basis for a changed business model. Only the packaging changes."
- Given I am evaluating the 90-day timeline, when I see the validation cycle, then I find the fixed review cadence (Day 14/30/60/90) with escalation steps at each checkpoint
